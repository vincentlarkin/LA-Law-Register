CHAPTER 4.  QUO WARRANTO
Art. 3901.  Definition
Quo warranto is a writ directing an individual to show by what authority he claims
or holds public office, or office in a corporation or limited liability company, or directing a
corporation or limited liability company to show by what authority it exercises certain
powers.  Its purpose is to prevent usurpation of office or of powers.
Acts 2017, No. 419, §1
