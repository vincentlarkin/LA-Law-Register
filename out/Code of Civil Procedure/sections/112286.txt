CHAPTER 2.  PETITION
Art. 891.  Form of petition
A.  The petition shall comply with Articles 853, 854, and 863, and, whenever
applicable, with Articles 855 through 861. It shall set forth the name, surname, and domicile
of the parties; shall contain a short, clear, and concise statement of all causes of action arising
out of, and of the material facts of, the transaction or occurrence that is the subject matter of
the litigation; shall designate a physical address, not a post office box, and an email address
for receipt of service of all items involving the litigation; and shall conclude with a prayer
for judgment for the relief sought. Relief may be prayed for in the alternative.
B.  For petitions involving domestic violence brought pursuant to R.S. 46:2131 et
seq., R.S. 9:361 et seq., Children's Code Article 1564 et seq., or Code of Civil Procedure
Article 3601 et seq., the address and parish of the residence of each petitioner and each
person on whose behalf the petition is filed may remain confidential with the court.
