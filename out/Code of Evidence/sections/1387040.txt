Art. 1105.  Burden of proof; civil proceedings
A.  In any civil proceeding in which the defense of self-defense is raised, the
defendant shall have the burden to prove by a preponderance of the evidence that he acted
in self-defense.
B.  For the purposes of this Article, if the defense of self-defense is raised in a civil
proceeding by an authorized person as defined in R.S. 9:2793.12, the burden of proof shall
be on the party asserting the action to prove by a preponderance of the evidence that the
injury, death, or loss complained of was not caused by a justified use of force or self-defense
by the authorized person.
Acts 2024, No. 729, §1.
