Art. 3025. Termination by principal

            The principal may terminate the mandate and the authority of the mandatary at any time. A mandate in the interest of the principal, and also of the mandatary or of a third person, may be irrevocable, if the parties so agree, for as long as the object of the contract may require.

            Acts 1997, No. 261, §1, eff. Jan. 1, 1998; Acts 2025, No. 488, §1.
