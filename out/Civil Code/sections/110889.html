Art. 695. Relocation of servitude

            The owner of the enclosed estate has no right to the relocation of the servitude of passage after it is fixed. The owner of the servient estate has the right to demand relocation of the servitude to a more convenient place at his own expense, provided that the new location is equally convenient to the enclosed estate.

            Acts 1977, No. 514, §1; Acts 2025, No. 27, §1.
