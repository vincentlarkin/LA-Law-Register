Art. 2035.  Rights of third person in good faith
Nullity of a contract does not impair the rights acquired through an onerous contract
by a third person in good faith.
If the contract involves immovable property, the principles of recordation apply to
a third person acquiring an interest in the property whether by onerous or gratuitous title.
Acts 1984, No. 331, §1, eff. Jan. 1, 1985; Acts 2005, No. 169, §2, eff. July 1, 2006;
Acts 2005, 1st Ex. Sess., No. 13, §1, eff. Nov. 29, 2005; Acts 2025, No. 488, §1.
