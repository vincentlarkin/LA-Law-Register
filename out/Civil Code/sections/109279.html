Art. 2021. Rights of third person in good faith

            Dissolution of a contract does not impair the rights acquired through an onerous contract by a third person in good faith.

            If the contract involves immovable property, the principles of recordation apply to a third person acquiring an interest in the property whether by onerous or gratuitous title.

            Acts 1984, No. 331, §1, eff. July 1, 1985; Acts 2005, No. 169, §2, eff. Jan. 1, 2006; Acts 2005, 1st Ex. Sess., No. 13, §1, eff. Nov. 29, 2005; Acts 2025, No. 488, §1.
