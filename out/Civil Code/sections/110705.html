Art. 520. Transfer of ownership by merchant

            Except as otherwise provided by legislation, a transferee in good faith and for fair value acquires ownership of a corporeal movable from a transferor who is not the owner only if the transferor has possession of the thing with consent of the owner, is a merchant customarily selling similar things, and transfers the thing in the regular course of the transferor's business.

            Acts 2023, No. 401, §1.
