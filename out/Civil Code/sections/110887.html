Art. 693. Enclosed estate; voluntary act

            If an estate becomes enclosed as a result of a voluntary act of its owner at the time of the enclosure, the neighbors are not bound to furnish a passage to the owner or the owner's successors.

            Acts 1977, No. 514, §1; Acts 2025, No. 27, §1.
