TITLE IV
SEARCH WARRANTS
Art. 161.  Property subject to seizure
A.  Except as authorized by Article 163.1 or 163.2, a judge may issue a warrant
authorizing the search for and seizure of any thing within the territorial jurisdiction of the
court which:
(1)  Has been the subject of theft.
(2)  Is intended for use or has been used as a means of committing an offense.
(3)  May constitute evidence tending to prove the commission of an offense.
B.  A judge of a city court located in the city of Bastrop may, only with the consent
of the judicial district court, issue a warrant authorizing the search for and seizure of anything
within the territorial jurisdiction of the district court.
C.  A judge may also issue a search warrant in all other cases specifically provided
by law.  A justice of the peace may issue a search warrant only in those cases specifically
provided by law.
Acts 1993, No. 846, §1; Acts 2005, No. 38, §1; Acts 2011, 1st Ex. Sess., No. 16, §1;
Acts 2022, No. 384, §1.
