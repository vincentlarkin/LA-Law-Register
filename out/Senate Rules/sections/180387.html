Rule 12.10. Vote required to pass legislative instruments

           A. No bill or concurrent resolution shall be adopted unless at least a majority of the members elected to the Senate vote in favor thereof.

           B. No joint resolution shall be adopted unless two-thirds of the members elected to the Senate vote in favor thereof.

           C. Except as provided in Paragraph (D), a resolution may be adopted by a majority of the members present and voting, without the necessity of a roll call vote.

           D. A resolution introduced on the last day of a legislative session may be adopted only by a majority of the members present and voting by roll call vote.

           Amended by SR 5 of 2020 1st EX.
