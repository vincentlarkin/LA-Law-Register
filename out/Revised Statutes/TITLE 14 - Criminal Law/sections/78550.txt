§50.2.  Perpetration or attempted perpetration of certain crimes of violence against a victim
sixty-five years of age or older
The court in its discretion may sentence, in addition to any other penalty provided by
law, any person who is convicted of a crime of violence or of an attempt to commit any of
the crimes as defined in R.S. 14:2(B) with the exception of first degree murder (R.S. 14:30),
second degree murder (R.S. 14:30.1), aggravated assault (R.S. 14:37), aggravated or first
degree rape (R.S. 14:42), and aggravated kidnapping (R.S. 14:44) to an additional three
years' imprisonment when the victim of such crime is sixty-five years of age or older at the
time the crime is committed.
Acts 2001, No. 648, §1; Acts 2015, No. 184, §1.
