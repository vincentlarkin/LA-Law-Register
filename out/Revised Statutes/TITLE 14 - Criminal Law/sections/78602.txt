§65.  Simple robbery
A.  Simple robbery is the taking of anything of value belonging to another from the
person of another or that is in the immediate control of another by use of force or
intimidation but not armed with a dangerous weapon.
B.  Whoever commits the crime of simple robbery shall be fined not more than three
thousand dollars, imprisoned with or without hard labor for not more than seven years, or
both.
Acts 1983, No. 70, §1; Acts 2022, No. 731, §1; Acts 2024, No. 119, §1.
