§1286.  Automobile insurance policies; increase in premium; age discrimination
An insurer shall not increase the premium charged for an automobile liability
insurance policy for any insured solely on the grounds that the insured has attained the age
of sixty-five or older.  Any insurer found to be in violation of this Section is subject to the
imposition of any penalty or regulatory action of the commissioner authorized pursuant to
the provisions of this Title.
Acts 1993, No. 205, §1; Redesignated from R.S. 22:635.2 by Acts 2008, No. 415, §1,
eff. Jan. 1, 2009; Acts 2025, No. 168, §1.
