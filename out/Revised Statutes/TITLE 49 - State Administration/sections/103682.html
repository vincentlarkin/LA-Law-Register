§251.2.  Authority to designate representative

The attorney general may designate a particular named assistant, special counsel or deputy to act for him and in his place at meetings of and in connection with the actions, transactions, functions and duties of agencies, boards, commissions or other political subdivisions of the state, or any subdivision thereof, including public districts and authorities.  

Added by Acts 1974, No. 398, §1.
