§130.  General and special meetings; how called

In its by-laws each association shall provide for one or more regular meetings annually.  The board of directors shall have the right to call a special meeting at any time, and ten per cent of the members or stockholders may file a petition stating the specific business to be brought before the association and demand a special meeting at any time. A special meeting shall thereupon be called by the directors.  Notice of all meetings, together with a statement of the purpose thereof, shall be mailed to each member at least five days prior to the meeting; provided, however, that the by-laws may require instead that notices may be given by publication in a newspaper of general circulation, published at the principal place of business of the association.
