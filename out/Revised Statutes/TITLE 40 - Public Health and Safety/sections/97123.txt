§1241.  Hospital prospective reimbursement methodology
A.  Notwithstanding any provisions of the Louisiana Administrative Code or any
contrary provision of any other law or regulation, for the purpose of the hospital prospective
reimbursement methodology and temporary licensure of international medical graduates, the
Louisiana Medical Assistance Program's recognition of a major teaching hospital is limited
to facilities having a documented affiliation agreement with a Louisiana medical school
accredited by the Liaison Committee on Medical Education or the Commission on
Osteopathic College Accreditation.
B.  A major teaching hospital shall meet one of the following criteria:
(1)  Be a major participant in at least four approved medical residency programs of
which at least two of the programs shall be in medicine, surgery, obstetrics and gynecology,
pediatrics, family practice, emergency medicine, or psychiatry.
(2)  Maintain an intern and resident full-time equivalency of at least twenty filled
positions with an approved medical residency program in family practice located more than
one hundred fifty miles from the medical school accredited by the Liaison Committee on
Medical Education.
C.  For the purposes of this Subpart, "hospital prospective reimbursement
methodology" means a method of hospital reimbursement in which Medicaid payment is
made based on a predetermined, fixed amount.
