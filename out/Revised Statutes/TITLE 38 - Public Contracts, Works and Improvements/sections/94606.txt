§1587.  Certificates of indebtedness
A.  The governing authority of any such drainage district complying with the provisions of this Part may issue, execute, negotiate, sell, and deliver negotiable interest-bearing coupon certificates of the district in an amount up to the total amount of the installments or deferred payments as provided in R.S. 38:1586.
B.  The certificates shall bear interest not to exceed the legal maximum limit, shall be payable annually, and shall mature serially over a period not exceeding twenty years, but in no event shall the certificates extend over a longer period of time than that provided for by the ordinance levying the local or special assessments which may be payable in annual installments.
C.  The certificates shall be of such form, date, and denomination and payable in principal and interest at such time and place as the governing authority may determine, and they shall be secured by and payable in principal and interest from the irrevocable pledge and dedication of the funds to be derived from the special assessments hereinabove provided which are payable in annual installments or deferred payments as provided for by this Part, which funds shall be set aside in a separate trust fund and shall not be drawn upon for any purpose other than to pay the principal and interest of the certificates.
Added by Acts 1983, No. 708, §1.
