§399.2.  Effect of paternity on pregnancy-related medical expenses
A.  The biological mother of a child may institute an action against the biological
father of the child to recover fifty percent of documented out-of-pocket pregnancy-related
medical expenses.  The biological mother may institute the action if the father has executed
an authentic act of acknowledgment or after the paternity of the child has been proven upon
proof by clear and convincing evidence as provided in Part I-A of this Chapter.
B.  Only the following out-of-pocket pregnancy-related medical expenses may be
recovered by the biological mother from the biological father:
(1)  Actual medical expenses, including hospital, testing, nursing, pharmaceutical,
travel, or other expenses, incurred by the biological mother for prenatal care and those
medical expenses incurred by the biological mother and child incident to birth.
(2)  Any additional expense authorized by order of the court upon a specific finding
that the expense is reasonable and necessary.
C.  The action to prove paternity for reimbursement of pregnancy-related expenses
by the biological mother shall be instituted within a peremptive period of two years from the
day of the birth of the child.
