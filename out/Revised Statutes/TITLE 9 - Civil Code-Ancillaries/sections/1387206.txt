§377.17.  Waiver and preclusion of privilege
A.  A privilege in accordance with R.S. 9:377.16 may be waived in a record or orally
during a proceeding if it is expressly waived by all parties and, in the case of the privilege
of a nonparty participant, it is also expressly waived by the nonparty participant.
B.  A person who makes a disclosure or representation about a collaborative family
law communication that prejudices another person in a proceeding may not assert a privilege
in accordance with R.S. 9:377.16, but this preclusion applies only to the extent necessary for
the person prejudiced to respond to the disclosure or representation.
Acts 2024, No. 98, §1.
