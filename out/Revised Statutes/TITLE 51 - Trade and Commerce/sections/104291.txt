§2247.  Public accommodations, resorts, amusements; discriminatory practices
prohibited
Except as otherwise provided in this Chapter, it is a discriminatory
practice for a person to deny an individual the full and equal enjoyment of the
goods, services, facilities, privileges, advantages, and accommodations of a
place of public accommodation, resort, or amusement, as defined in this
Chapter, on the grounds of race, creed, color, religion, sex, age, disability, as
defined in R.S. 51:2232, or national origin.
Acts 1988, No. 886, §1; Acts 1993, No. 820, §9.
