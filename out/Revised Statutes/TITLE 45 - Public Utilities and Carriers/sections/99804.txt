§1161.1.  Creation; composition; age; residence; domicile; qualifications; election; terms
A.  There shall be a Public Service Commission, which shall consist of five members,
who shall be qualified electors.  The members shall be elected from single-member districts,
established as provided by law.
B.  An elector who at the time of qualification as a candidate has attained the age of
eighteen years, resided in the state for the preceding two years, and been actually domiciled
for the preceding year in the single-member district established by law from which he seeks
election is eligible for election to the commission.
C.  The members shall be elected at the congressional elections for overlapping terms
of six years, and shall take office and begin their terms on the first day of January following
election.
