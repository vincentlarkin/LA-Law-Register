§2371.  Privileged communications

No interpreter/transliterator for the deaf is permitted, without consent of the person making the communication, to disclose any communication made in confidence by one seeking situation specific service, or any information that may have been obtained by reason of being such interpreter/transliterator.  

Added by Acts 1982, No. 135, §1.  Acts 1988, No. 922, §1.
